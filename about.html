Reflections

A Geometric Game To Reflect On

The Concept

Lasers are converging on a central core. You have one job: place mirrors to deflect them away. Every round gives you a set of geometric mirrors -- squares, triangles, hexagons, trapezoids -- and a field of incoming lasers. Arrange your mirrors, hit launch, and watch the physics play out.

There is no time pressure during setup. Take as long as you need to study the angles and plan your defense. Once you launch, the game runs on its own -- your placement is your entire strategy.

How It Works

1
Study the board. Preview arrows show where lasers will fire and in what direction.
2
Place your mirrors. Drag them anywhere on the board. Rotate for precise angles. Snap edges together to build walls. Mirrors cannot overlap or enter forbidden zones.
3
Start. Lasers fire. Your mirrors deflect them. The core survives -- or it doesn't.
4
Survive. Your score is how long the core stays alive. Maximum survival is 5 minutes.

Fair by Design

Every configuration is randomly generated, but the total reflective surface area is always exactly 84 units. You might get many small mirrors or a few large ones -- the total is always the same. Your score reflects placement skill, not luck.
